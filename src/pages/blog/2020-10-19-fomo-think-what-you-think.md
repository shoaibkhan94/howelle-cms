It's a regular Friday night, you're scrolling through people's stories on Instagram.

"Ah yes! This cafe looks fun, even I wanna have a cup of coffee there",

"oh! they are both together now? It'd be fun to have someone by their side",

"Damn, they all went to the new night club then? I wish I wasn't working late today, wonder what I missed on!"

A regular Friday night, and some regular thoughts. Aren't they?

But why are we discussing this? What difference will it make in anyone's life in knowing what we think of on a regular Friday night?

It's you. These thoughts and their consequences will make a difference in your lifestyle. The millennials are so used to these thoughts, that they don't find anything wrong in it. It's their drive to explore and do new things. The extent of this drive and the thought process it leads to can sometimes become harmful if the mind isn't kept in check.

FOMO, the fear of missing out is "a pervasive apprehension that others might be having rewarding experience from which one is absent"

It is in having these mere thoughts which lead to overthinking and hence serious mental conditions such as anxiety and depression.

For eg. A person may just want to be a part of a certain group. Once he is not invited to a group activity by the same group he may start thinking as to 'why they did not involve him?', 'What he must have missed?', 'What inside jokes will he never be a part of now?'. Right when he teaches his mind to think this way, his mind will start giving him ideas as to what and not what he should, do to be in the group or to leave it, what changes he needs to make in himself to feel the inclusivity from their side.

If this ain't Anxiety then what is?

If this won't lead to low self-esteem, lack of interest in activities, and hence depression, then what will?

THINK. THINK ABOUT YOUR THINKING.
