How many times have you mistakenly liked a picture from a century ago, while looking at a social media profile? Ever wondered "oh what are they up to these days!?”, and went on to type their name in the search bar?

Looking at your crush, ex-lover, friends or even random strangers on the internet has become so common these days that we do it without a thought. We often go from one profile to another and then onto the next one.

The question is, will you consider it as 'stalking'?

Generally, we use the term 'stalking' for simply looking at social media profiles. Whereas stalking is "the act of following a person or animal as closely as possible without being seen or heard" or "the crime of illegally following and watching someone over a period of time", as given by the Cambridge Dictionary.

Is stalking actually a crime? What goes on in the mind of a stalker? Can stalking be considered as a psychological disorder?

Stalking is a behaviour. It can’t necessarily be categorized as a psychological disorder. A stalker stalks due to many reasons, one of which can be a mental health problem or a disorder like depression, substance abuse, and personality disorders. The reason as to why a stalker does what it does can be found out by listing a few types of stalkers.

**Obsessed**: As the name suggests, these kinds are obsessed with the person they idolize. They constantly think about that individual. These kinds of stalkers are just a casual acquaintance to the individual. They might as well most probably be suffering from loneliness.

**Rejected**: These stalkers have been a part of a challenging relationship in the past. The controlling and abusive one in nature. They find it difficult to share their feeling with others and communicating about the rejection and the problems faced in the past relationship(s), which leads them to feel the need to keep a check on their current partners.

**Incompetent**: These kinds are usually looking for partners. They do not go with the usual “talking and dating” flow of action. They indeed adopt ways which are rather creepy i.e. stalking.

**Intelligent**: The planners of the group, the intelligent stalkers fall into this category. These people plan and prepare for their stalking behaviour and/ or patterns. These stalkers can meet the criteria of being a sociopath. They are very skilled in planning the “attack” and controlling others with their loveliness.

**Motivated**: They believe that the person they idolize is the only person they could ever love. These stalkers remain motivated with this thought and thinking and hence, stalk.

In conclusion, as we read above, a person stalks because of a reason, the cause of which has to be found out and hence, helped with. It’d be better if we stop looking at stalkers as criminals and rather look at them as someone who needs help. No number of fines or charges are going to make them stop with this “behaviour” whereas, a solution to their mental problem, the insecurity or the thinking may.
