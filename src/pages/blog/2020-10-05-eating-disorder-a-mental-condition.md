Remember how you made your mother run behind you to make you eat the food? Or were you the kind of child who did not make faces when it came to food!? Whatever the case, is it still the same? or have you made changes in your food habits? Do you eat when what and how much you want?

If the answer to these questions was a 'yes', you might be suffering from an eating disorder. Now you may be wondering if it is that big of a deal or not, as there are a lot of other, more important problems people are going through, for example, mental illnesses like Depression and Anxiety.

Eating Disorders is a Mental Health Condition. How? you ask?

When an individual consumes more or less than the required amount of food, it can be considered as an eating disorder. A sudden change in the diet due to any given reason makes it prone to the problem of eating disorders.

Why would a person make changes in its diet? Let us discuss some causes of the same.

Relationship Stress due to draining or failed relationships with family, friends, or lovers can cause a change in the eating patterns. One might not feel like having food. In a different case scenario, a person might intake a lot more amount of food than normal, to keep their mind off the stress.

Body Image issue can be another reason which makes a person cut down on or add onto their diet. Our society has set a standardized "body shape". It wants everyone to look a certain way and believes that it is the 'only' way a person should look in order to be appealing. If they don't fulfill these criteria, they need to make a change. Most people, to make a change in how they look or more importantly, are perceived by the 'society', mess up their eating patterns, leading to eating disorders.

Another common cause of eating disorders is Genes. Twin adoption studies show, that when a child is diagnosed with eating disorders. There's a 50% chance of its twin getting the same problem too. Researches like these portray that Eating disorders, like many other serious diseases, are hereditary.

Apart from a change in the amount of food intake, this disorder can cause people to develop some other harmful eating habits. To study this furthermore, we shall now learn about the common types of eating disorders.



**1.Anorexia Nervosa**

People with this kind of eating disorder pay a great amount of attention to the food they eat. They monitor their calories intake very closely and tend to restrict themselves and eat a lot less than required for smooth body functioning. These people think of themselves as overweight even if they are dangerously underweight.

Anorexia can be of two subtypes — the restricting type and the binge eating and purging type.

**2. Bulimia nervosa**

Individuals suffering from bulimia eat large amounts of food in one go. Their binge eating sessions continue till they can’t even intake a single drop of water. These binge eating habits generally are for food items they may normally avoid. During the “Binge Eating Time”, they try to compensate for the calories not taken in the first place.

Patients of Bulimia Nervosa, unlike Anorexia, are not underweight. They do pay close attention to their eating patterns to control their weight and not go overweight.

**3. Binge eating disorder**

As the name suggests, people with this disorder have binge eating sessions, they have a lot of food in one go and can't control their food consumption during the same. Later, while thinking about their binge eating habits they feel distressed, guilt, or shame. They are often overweight or have obesity, which in turn increases the chances of other medical risks such as heart problems, type 2 diabetes, and stroke.

They do not have purging habits, like vomiting, fasting, or excessive exercises unlike the anorexia binge eating purging type.

**4. Pica**

Pica is a type of eating disorder in which people crave non-food items such as, ice, soil, hair, cloth, wool, pebbles laundry detergent, or cornstarch.

**5. Rumination disorder**

Rumination disorder is a fairly new type in this list. It is a condition in which an individual regurgitates food it has already chewed and swallowed, re-chews it, and then either re-swallows it or spits it out. This rumination generally occurs within the first 30 minutes after a meal. It can affect people at all stages of life and can be fatal if not treated.

**6. Avoidant/restrictive food intake disorder**

Individuals with this disorder avoid certain food items due to a lack of interest in eating or distaste for certain smells, tastes, colors, textures, or temperatures.

Up till now, you may have thought about what would be the ways to prevent or treat these disorders.

Prevention of the same can be done from an individual's very childhood. Parents need to make sure their child eats food sitting together with them so that it learns the proper amount of nutrition to consumed and hence continues this practice in the future. A family that is having its meals together automatically paves the way to makes sure that every member of the family is fed well as they can then keep a check on each other’s food patterns.

If the prevention processes haven't been performed and treatment is required, a patient should seek help from a psychologist, a nutritionist, or a dietician and in some cases, even a doctor.

This team together can help in motivating the patient to improve their eating patterns, provide them with a proper diet plan or medications, if required.

Eating Disorder is a type of mental problem that most people do not consider important or even if they do, they tend to shove away the thought of seeking help or even accepting that they are suffering from the same. If you feel that a friend is a having a problem with their eating habits, try and talk to them, make them understand that it is okay to accept, and seek help in order to lead a healthy life.
