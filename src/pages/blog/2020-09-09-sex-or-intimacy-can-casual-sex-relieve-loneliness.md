It's been quite a few times now that while scrolling through my Instagram feed, I come across a quote. The quote says "We don't want sex, we crave Intimacy". If you're a dedicated scroller and an avid thinker like me, you must have come across this too and must have thought to yourself, "Is this true?" "Doesn’t intimacy and sex go hand in hand?" "Wouldn't getting physical with someone automatically give a sense of intimacy?"

The answer to these questions lies in us, our minds, our psychology.

The Merriam Webster dictionary defines the word sex as "heterosexual intercourse involving penetration of the vagina by the penis." Quite biological, isn't it? Sexual contacts are ought to be biological. What gives them a sense of meaning (or in our words 'intimacy') is our mind. The relationship we share with our sexual partner outside the bedroom, the comfort level, the warmth and most importantly how we as individuals perceive sex and what it means to us.

Then why do most people go for "no strings attached" or "casual hook-ups" over a dedicated relationship these days? Is the "hook-up culture" taking over upon us? What gets us drawn to it? It can be easy access to sexual pleasure, without giving in the efforts required for a serious commitment. It can also be the fact that the "physical needs" are to be fulfilled once the body gets used to the sexual contacts from a past relationship.

Talking of past relationships, on a psychological level, an individual may try to fill a void caused by a sour past. A person may try to make up for the missing warmth and love through sexual passion. Wonder why Kabir Singh was "Kabir Singh" after his lover got married to another man?

‘Intimacy’ in general means ‘closeness”. It can further be divided into emotional and physical intimacy. Closeness in thoughts and emotions, i.e closeness of mind is emotional intimacy. On the other hand, closeness in touch and feel is physical intimacy. We often get confused between the two and try to cover the gap caused by one through the other. Going back to our Bollywood reference, it's quite clear that in the movie Kabir Singh, the protagonist was longing for every bit of the relationship shared with his past lover. Yet was trying to fill in the 'emotional void' by physical means.

Are Dating apps like Tinder used to provide a physical intimacy to people actually longing for emotional intimacy?

Because these apps are generally used by youngsters, you may argue their sole purpose is indeed just casual sex and nothing emotional. Apps like "Gleeden", an extra-marital app, are gaining popularity these days too. What can be the cause of this rise? If the reason behind casual sex is just the physical part of it, then these many married individuals would not have been infidel to their partners tapping the install button for the mere pleasure of a few seconds. There's more likely, a greater reason, i.e emotional intimacy. In today's time, most of us are busy building our lives. The career, the house we live in, the clothes we wear, the kind of food we eat. We get so busy in caring for these things that we forget about the parts of our lives which need actual care - our emotions, our relations, our partners and their emotions. This leads to the instability in our emotional intimacy and hence- a longing.

Considering our discussion up till now, we can conclude that individuals do look up to casual sex to feel a sense of completeness and to fight loneliness. The question which now arises is that "does casual sex relieve loneliness?" and if it does, then for how long?

In 2014, a study published in the Journal of Sex Research looked at single, heterosexual college students age 18 to 25. The researchers found that casual sex was negatively associated with positive psychological and positively correlated with stress. Based on this, the research team concluded, "For emerging-adult college students, engaging in casual sex may elevate the risk for negative psychological outcomes."

Sex indeed is a great distraction for human beings. It can be used to distract our mind from a stressful situation. Then why does the study say that casual sex raises the risk of stress and negativity? Casual sex provides closeness only at the time spent inside the bedroom. The feelings of the touch, warmth and intimacy, ends right when the sexual intercourse is over. An individual looking for distraction may just be distracted for that particular time. Similarly, a person confused between physical and emotional intimacy will feel the ‘intimacy’ just for that particular time. When it ends, their minds will feel more drained, finding it hard to face the fact that it was all “temporary” and none of it will provide a permanent solution to their stress or a sense of completeness. Hence, a person trying to relieve loneliness through casual sex may not be completely successful even if it causes a relief, it won’t be for a long time.

Another study, conducted by Owen, J., Fincham, F.D. & Moore, J, which researched on young adults, hooking up over the past year, positive reactions to prior hook-ups, alcohol use, and loneliness were associated with hooking up over a 4-month period. Individuals with more depressive symptoms and feelings of loneliness at Time 1 and subsequently engaged in penetrative hook-ups reported fewer depressive symptoms and lower feelings of loneliness at Time 2 as compared to young adults who did not hook up. However, young adults who reported fewer depressive symptoms and were less lonely at Time 1 and engaged in penetrative hook-ups over the 4-month period reported more depressive symptoms and greater feelings of loneliness at Time 2 as compared to young adults who did not hook up.

Looking at both the studies, we can conclude that different sets of individuals react differently to casual sex. Some find it useful and mentally fulfilling while some find it draining. Earlier, we discussed about people who may find it draining, let’s now try and find out how are people benefiting from casual sex on a mental level.

At the beginning of this article, I mentioned that it's all in our minds and the way we perceive sex. If an individual considers sexual intercourse just like any other physical activity that the body needs to perform after reaching a certain age, casual sex may not harm their mental peace. Even if the person is just trying out casual sex for expanding its horizons and experiencing new things, casual sex may likely not harm such an individual too.

On the other hand, if an individual considers sexual intercourse as the next step to an intense and deep emotional connection, i.e they get their feelings and emotions involved when it comes to sex. Casual sex, in this case, may play a great role in causing stress and instability in the mental health.

The perception of sex depends on the upbringing, education and experiences of a person. When it comes to mind and thoughts, no two individuals are the same. A hundred minds may have a hundred different perceptions which lead to the way they understand and act upon things. Casual Sex is no exception.
